Why?

Air pollution is termed by many as a “silent killer” and causes many serious long-term respiratory problems, especially for people residing in cities. The members of our team come from countries where this is a striking, hard-to-miss, everyday reality. It is clear that quick action needs to be taken but there is quite a confusion as to how to drive this change. The theoretical solutions are plentiful, but due to the lack of evidence of their valid effects based on empirical data, a lot of time is squandered on abstract debates. We are going to contribute towards accelerating this change to provide future generations one of nature’s most valuable gifts: a breath of fresh air.

How?

The ground measurements of air pollution within a city or its outskirts will be extended with the help of machine learning algorithms to have a larger and detailed coverage area. Coupling the power of satellital data with local air pollution data will be done to ensure a better reliability of data. Our simulation platform will visualize the effect of various government policies on the microclimate of cities. It can mainly be used by policy makers to communicate the benefits of their proposed policies to the public.
